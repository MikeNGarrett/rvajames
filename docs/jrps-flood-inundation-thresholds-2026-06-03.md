# JRPS Per-Location Flood Inundation Thresholds — 2026-06-03

Follow-up to `docs/jrps-research-2026-06-02.md` deep-research workflow.
That workflow surfaced the Richmond-Westham flood inundation ArcGIS
Experience as the most authoritative source for per-location gauge
thresholds but couldn't produce specific values from the published
front-end alone. This pass goes after the underlying data directly.

**Status: provisional.** Three model-based thresholds derived with

## The source

[Richmond Partner FIM](https://experience.arcgis.com/experience/8d12656d99224ac4b599fb270f4d199f/page/Richmond-Westham) —
ArcGIS Experience Builder app published by `NWS.AKQ_noaa` (NWS
Wakefield, VA). Inundation polygons + depth rasters generated by:

> USACE NAO, using HEC-RAS model and developed GIS tools by NAO. The
> work is completed in coordination with the NWS - Wakefield Forecast
> Office and U.S. Geological Survey - Virginia Water Science Center.
> The inundation products are generated for the following USGS Gages
> 02037500 [Westham] and 02037705 [City Locks].

The experience has three pages: **Richmond Locks**, **Richmond
Westham**, and **Source Information**. Each gauge has its own FIM
(set of stage-keyed inundation maps) clipped to that gauge's
influence area.

## How to query it programmatically

The published front-end is an Experience Builder app that loads its
config from ArcGIS Online. The config exposes:

- Two webmaps, one per gauge page:
  - **Richmond Westham FIM**: itemId `d2fd3ce625614b508ca0aed38e6e6b21`
  - **Richmond Locks FIM**: itemId `7cdf21aecdf247c2acb918c7c01f600f`
- Each webmap's `operationalLayers` list every stage layer with the
  human-readable label ("16 ft.", "22 ft. (Major Flood Stage)") and
  the underlying MapServer URL.

Fetch the webmap data with no auth required:

```sh
curl -sL "https://noaa.maps.arcgis.com/sharing/rest/content/items/d2fd3ce625614b508ca0aed38e6e6b21/data?f=json"
```

That returns a JSON document listing all 18 Westham stage layers
(11 ft through 28 ft).

### Service naming convention — important

The MapServer slugs use NAVD88 water-surface elevation × 10, NOT
gauge-stage feet. The Westham gauge zero datum is ≈ 98.4 ft NAVD88,
so:

| Westham gauge stage | Layer slug         | WSE NAVD88 |
|---------------------|--------------------|------------|
| 11 ft               | `elev_109_8`       | 109.8 ft   |
| 12 ft (Minor Flood) | `elev_110_8_new`   | 110.8 ft   |
| 15 ft (Mod. Flood)  | `elev_113_8`       | 113.8 ft   |
| 22 ft (Major Flood) | `elev_120_8`       | 120.8 ft   |
| 25 ft               | `elev_123_8`       | 123.8 ft   |
| 28 ft (Record)      | `elev_126_4_3`     | 126.4 ft   |

The City Locks set uses a different naming pattern (`elev_008` →
`elev_028`) — those numbers ARE gauge stages directly for the City
Locks gauge.

**Don't conflate the two sets.** If you query the City Locks layers
for a point in the Westham reach (e.g. Pony Pasture, Belle Isle),
you'll get all-dry results because the polygon is clipped to the
downstream reach.

## Probe technique

Each MapServer is `capabilities: "Map,TilesOnly,Tilemap"` — no
`/query` or `/identify` endpoint. The only API is tile fetching:

```
https://tiles.arcgis.com/tiles/C8EMgrsFcRFL6LrL/arcgis/rest/services/{slug}/MapServer/tile/{z}/{y}/{x}
```

To test "is location (lat, lng) inundated at stage X":

1. Convert (lat, lng) to Web Mercator tile coordinates at zoom z.
2. Fetch the tile for the stage-X MapServer at those coordinates.
3. Three response shapes:
   - **HTTP 404** — tile not seeded at this zoom (sparse cache,
     fall through to a coarser zoom)
   - **HTTP 200, ~872 byte body** — empty transparent placeholder.
     Means the inundation polygon doesn't extend into this tile at
     all. Effectively dry.
   - **HTTP 200, ≥ ~1 kB body** — real PNG tile with polygon
     coverage. Check the pixel at the location's offset within the
     tile; opaque (alpha > 0) = inundated.
4. Repeat for stages 11, 12, …, 28. The lowest stage where the pixel
   goes opaque is the threshold for that location.

The probe script is checked in at `scripts/probe-fim.py` (see below)
and uses Pillow for tile pixel reading. It tries zoom 18 first and
falls back to lower zooms on 404.

## Cross-check: Ancarrow's Landing

Probed (37.5184, -77.4207) against the City Locks set as a sanity
check. Result: **first wet at 15 ft City Locks gauge**. The polygon
expands consistently (15 → 28 ft City Locks: tile bytes 18 kB → 48 kB).
This is consistent with Ancarrow's known low elevation along the
river bank near the slave trail terminus, and validates that the
probe technique correctly identifies actually-flooding terrain.

## Confidence rationale

The "high confidence" label on the three threshold values means
high confidence in the **mechanical correctness** of the probe — the
pixel I sampled is genuinely opaque (or transparent) in the tile
returned by the official USACE/NWS service for that stage. The
underlying HEC-RAS model's accuracy is a separate question; flood
inundation maps generally have ±0.5 ft to ±1 ft uncertainty per FEMA
guidance, and water-surface elevation between the gauge and the
location of interest can diverge by feet over miles of river.

Specifically, do **not** treat these as hard cutoffs. The recommended
use in the dashboard is "model predicts inundation at or above stage
X; treat lower stages as approaching-but-not-yet."

The "needs visual cross-check" gap is the asymmetry between
"polygon doesn't reach my pixel" (could be true or could be a
coordinate slop issue) and "polygon does reach my pixel" (definitive).
Chrome UI validation in the next sub-task lets us look at the map
visually and resolve the asymmetry.

## Chrome UI validation pass (added 2026-06-03)

Followed up the tile probe by driving the same ArcGIS Experience
through Chrome MCP. The Experience uses a per-stage *layer list*
(not a single slider), where each row toggles a specific stage's
inundation polygon on/off. The basemap renders the polygon as
semi-transparent light-blue overlay; toggling stages on and off
shows the polygon expanding/contracting against the satellite
imagery.

What the Chrome UI confirmed:

- **Westham FIM geographic extent**: the polygon at 28 ft Record
  Flood visibly spans from the far west (Country Club of Virginia /
  Tuckahoe area, well upstream of Pony Pasture) all the way east to
  ~Mayo Island. East of Mayo Island the polygon ends — Shiplock
  Trail, Ancarrow's Landing, and Rocketts Landing are NOT inside
  the Westham polygon at any stage. This corroborates the tile
  probe's "Shiplock dry through 28 ft Westham" result and explains
  it as a model-coverage boundary, not a real-world dry signal.

- **Pony Pasture IS in the Westham polygon at high stages**: at
  28 ft the polygon clearly extends through the Pony Pasture /
  Stratford Hills area on the south bank. This CONTRADICTS the tile
  probe's "Pony Pasture dry through 28 ft" result, which was almost
  certainly a coordinate-sampling miss. The polygon is narrow at
  this section of river (a few hundred meters wide at most stages),
  so the probe's 3-pixel neighborhood at z=16 may have landed just
  off the polygon edge. The model DOES place Pony Pasture inside
  the inundation extent at major flood.

- **Brown's Island wet at 11 ft (lowest modeled stage)**: at 11 ft
  the polygon visibly traces the river channel through downtown
  including the area immediately surrounding Brown's Island. The
  island itself stays dry at this stage (it's elevated above
  channel water), but the riverbank pixel sampled by the probe
  reads correctly as wet.

- **Belle Isle entirely under polygon at 22 ft Major Flood**: the
  visible 22 ft polygon completely covers the Belle Isle landmass
  including the south-channel rock area. The exact 20 ft threshold
  from the probe was not visually re-confirmed at single-foot
  precision (per-foot toggling is high-friction in the UI), but
  the broader pattern matches.

- **Downtown coverage at 22 ft**: polygon visibly inundates the
  Tredegar area, Manchester floodwall walkway, and the
  Mayo/Belle Islands. Potterfield Bridge midspan was not visually
  inspected at single-foot precision.

What the Chrome UI did NOT resolve:

- Single-foot precision for any of the four locations. The layer-
  list toggling pattern means each per-stage verification requires
  one click + 1-3 s tile load + 1 screenshot, and screenshot
  permissions require explicit user approval per call. A full
  cycle of 18 stages × 5 locations × 3 zoom levels = 270+
  permission prompts. Not a reasonable use of the validation
  channel.

- Whether the probe's specific Belle Isle 20 ft and Potterfield
  25 ft numbers are exactly right. The UI confirms they're in
  the right ballpark (Belle Isle definitely wet at 22 ft Major;
  Potterfield definitely below the inundation at moderate stages,
  reached at major-flood-plus stages) but doesn't pin specific
  feet.

Net effect: the Chrome pass **corrects one finding** (Pony Pasture
IS in the Westham FIM polygon at higher stages, contrary to the
probe's "dry through 28 ft" result) and **confirms the others
directionally** (Brown's Island wet at low stages, Belle Isle wet
at moderate/major stages, Shiplock outside Westham extent).
